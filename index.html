The real barrier to data careers

Breaking into data is broken
for most career changers

Data careers are high-growth, high-paying, and increasingly in-demand. But the path is broken. Generic bootcamps assume coding experience, price out anyone with constraints, and leave you completely alone when it gets hard. When confusion hits, there's no one to say "ignore that for now", so people conclude they're not smart enough. They were never told the wall was the course, not them.

1 in 3

Up to one in three bootcamp graduates remain completely unemployed in their target field six full months after completion. Accelerated courses often fail to build deep expertise, leaving job-seekers stranded in a highly competitive market.

$10k–$17k+

The true average upfront cost of a data bootcamp. Despite the massive investment, these programs systematically skip critical mathematical and architectural fundamentals, leaving graduates fragile and underprepared for real enterprise data problems.

The Proof Gap

Even career changers who finish a course have nothing concrete to show for it. A certificate from a platform with 50,000 identical reviews means nothing to a hiring manager. Without real projects and a portfolio, finishing is indistinguishable from not starting.

The Confidence Trap

Most people don't quit because the content is too hard. They quit because hitting a wall, a SQL join, a Python error, feels like evidence they're not smart enough. They were never told that confusion is normal. That the wall is the course, not them.
