The real barrier to data careers

Breaking into data is broken
for most career changers

Data careers are high-growth and increasingly in-demand. But the path is broken. Generic bootcamps assume coding experience, price out anyone with constraints, and leave you completely alone when it gets hard. People quit not because the content is too hard, but because there's no one to say "ignore that for now."

1 in 3

Bootcamp graduates remain unemployed in their target field six months after completion. Accelerated courses often fail to build deep expertise, leaving job-seekers stranded.

$10k–$17k+

The average upfront cost of a data bootcamp. Despite the investment, most programs skip critical fundamentals, leaving graduates underprepared for real enterprise work.

The proof gap

Even career changers who finish a course have nothing concrete to show. A certificate from a platform with 50,000 identical reviews means nothing to a hiring manager.

The confidence trap

Most people quit because hitting a wall feels like evidence they're not smart enough. They were never told that confusion is normal. The wall is the course, not them.
